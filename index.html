About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Applied Mathematics and Computational Science at the University of Pennsylvania. Concurrently, I am pursuing a Master's degree in Statistics and Data Science at Wharton. I have the privilege of being advised by Professor Joshua Plotkin and Professor Weijie Su.

My research interests primarily focus on employing mathematical and computational methods to examine law and social dynamics, including investigating implications of and solutions to the legal and ethical challenges posed by technological advancements.

Before beginning my graduate studies, I practiced law and served in the government of Japan, after earning my LL.B. from Keio University.
